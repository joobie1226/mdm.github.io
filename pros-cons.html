Chrome

Pros:

  • Has extensions for your favorite applications. For example, you can embed the spellchecker or AdBlocker
  • It's the best adaptive variant for working with Google services (Google Disk, Google Documents, etc.), and it works perfectly with these applications, both in computer and mobile versions
  • As explained earlier, Google Chrome easily syncs your browser history, bookmarks, passwords, and settings on all of your devices when you log in to your Google account

Cons:

  • It doesn't support such useful features as a built-in VPN, cryptocurrency locker, and reading mode
  • Chrome is known to have privacy issues, having had occasions of large user data leaks

Safari

Pros:

  • Has an excellent and user-friendly interface
  • It's the best existing browser for Apple devices, designed to save your MacBook's or iPhone's battery.
  • This browser was the first to integrate a Reading mode feature that allows you to read the articles without being distracted by ads or videos.

Cons:

  • You can't use it if you don't have Mac products or an emulator.

Edge

Pros:

  • Minimum battery drain,the browser doesn't use much disk memory, which allows it to perform better.
  • Every article on the internet can become an audiobook because of Immersive Reader mode, which reads the web pages aloud with lifelike voices

Cons:

  • It has a few extensions but allows for the use of Google plug-ins
